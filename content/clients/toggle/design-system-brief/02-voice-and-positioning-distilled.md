# Toggle voice & positioning — distilled for the design system

> Distilled from `brain/voice/toggle-house-voice.md`, `brain/positioning/agency-profile.md`, `brain/positioning/differentiators.md`. Read these in source if more detail needed — they live in the Toggle Brain repo.

## The brand in one paragraph

**Toggle Solutions** is a Malaysia/ASEAN digital growth partner positioned as **Technical Growth Architecture** — not a traditional marketing agency. The differentiator is *diagnosis-first*: Toggle benchmarks metrics **outside** marketing scope (retail footfall conversion, sales first-response time, website bounce, CRM workflow gaps) before recommending a single channel or spending a dollar on creative. The company refuses retainers when the real problem isn't marketing. Tagline: *Your Digital Growth Partner*. One-liner: *Eliminating silos. Unifying the digital ecosystem. We integrate performance, tech, and creative into a single engine for sustainable growth.*

## The five voice adjectives (these drive design decisions too)

Each adjective produces ONE testable design rule. Not a vibe — a rule the team applies before shipping anything.

1. **Direct** — gets to the point. No throat-clearing. **Design rule:** one CTA per surface; one decision per screen. If the prospect can't name the next action in under 3 seconds, redesign.
2. **Honest** — tells clients what they need to hear, not what they want. Includes the contrarian "we turned down the retainer" framing. **Design rule:** the **strikethrough rewrite** is Toggle's signature rhetorical pattern — applied to hero, service descriptions, pricing, team framing, case-study challenges, OOH. See `01-brand-dna-snapshot.md` §9.2 for 6 worked examples.
3. **Sharp** — confident, specific, numeric. "47% CPL reduction" not "dramatically lower costs." **Design rule:** numerals at display weight; specific over vague; mono-numeric stats not animated counters.
4. **Experienced** — speaks like a senior operator who's seen patterns across industries. **Design rule (positive, not vague):** *every assertion carries its receipt inline* — stat next to claim, source next to quote, named operator next to expertise claim. No decoupled "trust us" copy. No appendices for proof.
5. **Unpretentious** — confident without arrogance; no buzzwords, no jargon for jargon's sake. **Design rule (positive, not just negation):** *nothing decorative survives review.* Every line, shape, color is load-bearing. Architectural-drawing discipline — every line means something. If you can delete it without losing meaning, delete it.

## The five differentiators

1. **We focus on what drives profit.** Not vanity metrics. Judged on revenue, qualified pipeline, cost-to-acquire.
2. **Senior operators only.** Every practice led by senior operators who work directly with clients. No junior account-manager layer.
3. **Everything under one partner.** Acquisition, conversion, retention on the same team, same data, same accountability.
4. **Cross-industry thinking.** Patterns from higher-ed transferred to insurance; e-commerce patterns applied to banking.
5. **Long-term growth, not short-term spikes.** Compounding gains — authority, infrastructure, creative libraries, retention systems.

## What Toggle never sounds like (and never looks like)

- Corporate and vague — "results-driven solutions for sustainable growth"
- Hype-driven — "INSANE ROAS 🔥🔥🔥"
- Humble-bragging without data
- Preachy or self-righteous
- Generic marketing agency — "we're passionate about your brand"
- Overly technical without context
- Trend-chasing without substance

**Visual equivalents to avoid:**
- Generic agency neon-purple gradient backgrounds
- Stock photography of "diverse team smiling at laptop"
- Over-decorated bento grids with no info hierarchy
- Aggressive glossy 3D renders
- Y2K / AI-startup neon-on-black tropes
- Whimsical illustration styles (mascots, characters, blob shapes)
- Emoji in marketing surfaces

## Platform voice → platform design

| Platform | Voice | Design rule |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (primary)** | Direct, analytical, peer-to-peer between operators. Contrarian takes welcome. | Stat-card-led visuals. Big-number first. Dark navy + electric blue. Toggle wordmark bottom-left. No emoji. |
| **Instagram** | Punchy, hook-first, confidence without polish. | Square + 4:5. Big type. One insight per slide in a 5–10 slide carousel. |
| **TikTok / Shorts** | 30s version of the LinkedIn take. One data point, one principle. | Vertical 9:16. Lower-third with stat + source. Operator-pov shots. No music swells. |
| **Website** | Operator speaking frankly. Reasoning visible. | Dark mode default. Negative space generous. Strikethrough hero. CTA pill button. |
| **Proposals/Decks** | Senior strategist in a room. | Light mode for proposals; dark for decks. Stat cards mandatory. Bracket watermark. |

## The "Zaid voice" (founder/strategist POV — applies to thought-leadership content)

- First-person, specific, numerical ("48 hours," "RM35 CPL," "32,000 leads")
- Intellectually honest ("we turned down the retainer because…")
- Not promotional — the insight is the product
- Contrarian when warranted ("most agencies would've taken the money")
- Systems thinker — connects specific observations to broader patterns
- Malaysia/ASEAN context present but not constantly stated

**Design implication:** when content is Zaid-bylined (long-form LinkedIn, founder posts, podcast clips), the template should be quieter than the Toggle-bylined stat cards — fewer graphics, more typographic emphasis, the operator's words doing the work.

## Brand values that should be visible in the system

1. **Honesty over comfort** — show real metrics, don't sanitize
2. **System thinking over channel thinking** — design components as parts of a system, not isolated stickers
3. **Results over activity** — every template foregrounds outcomes
4. **Earned expertise** — opinions come from experience; visuals are confident, not hedging
5. **Long-term over short-term** — the system should age well (no trend-chasing visuals that look dated in 18 months)
