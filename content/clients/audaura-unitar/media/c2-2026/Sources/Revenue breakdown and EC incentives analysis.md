# Revenue breakdown and EC incentives analysis

## Business Context & Revenue Model
* UC/RC campuses only recognized for homegrown program revenue (not online programs)
* Revenue split: 40% new enrollment, 60% recurring students
* Sales team (Education Counselors) compensated 60% fixed, 40% variable based on points system
    * Homegrown UC programs: 2 points
    * Online UIU programs: 1 point
    * Target: ~120 points total
* Current achievement: 40% homegrown, 60% online programs
* New requirement: ECs must clear hurdle rate on homegrown programs before online recognition

## Geographic Performance Issues
* Three underperforming states: Penang, Malacca, Kuching
* Penang location in Bayan Lepas (industrial area, not tourist zone)
* Malacca campus in Jonker Street (parking/accessibility issues)
* Competition from state-owned universities significantly impacts demand
    * Example: Terengganu has 2 large state universities vs Kelantan
    * Kelantan: 1,500 students, Terengganu: <400 students despite 20% more school leavers

## Campaign Strategy Restructuring
* Moving away from 34,000 lead volume target to focus on UC locations
* Need to over-index spending in UC areas (similar to current KL overspend approach)
* Two potential approaches:
    1. Ensure spend/volume from all focus locations regardless of CPL
    2. Categorize UCs by priority tiers and allocate spend accordingly
* Expected 30% higher CPL in UC locations due to smaller markets and local competition
* Algorithm will favor lower CPL areas, requiring separate campaign structure

## Creative & Targeting Requirements
* Homegrown programs fairly consistent across 10 UC locations: ECE, business diploma, multimedia
* Creative strategy: One base creative with 6 variations showing different UC names/locations
* Geographic targeting: State-focused rather than cross-border (exception: Terengganu-Pahang border)
* Language considerations and potential state flag incorporation
* Need more MBA creatives for TikTok campaign scaling
