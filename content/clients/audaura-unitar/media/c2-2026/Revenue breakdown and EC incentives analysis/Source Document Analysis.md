## 1. Revenue & Incentive Misalignment

The core structural problem: UC/RC campus revenue recognition only counts homegrown programs, but ECs are currently hitting targets 60% through online UIU programs (1 point each) rather than homegrown UC programs (2 points each). The variable pay structure is rewarding the wrong activity.

| Metric | Current Reality | Required Direction |
|---|---|---|
| EC point mix | 40% homegrown / 60% online | Reverse to homegrown majority |
| Revenue recognition | Only homegrown counts for campus P&L | Must drive homegrown NE |
| New hurdle rate | Not yet defined | Must be set before presenting to sales team |

**Risk**: The new hurdle rate will reduce EC take-home variable pay in the short term, creating sales team resistance. The Thursday C1/C2 plan review needs to include a clear transition plan and revised expectations.

### Critical Finding: Selangor Is Distorting Everything
Selangor at **RM36 CPL** makes the blended average look healthy. Strip it out:
- Remaining 11 locations: ~RM853K spend for ~6,421 leads = **RM133 avg CPL**
- That is 34% above the RM99 blended plan target
- Algorithm is naturally dumping budget into Selangor (Kelana Jaya) because it has the lowest CPL
- This is the exact structural problem identified in the strategy doc — separate campaign structure required

### TikTok CPL Problem
TikTok planned CPL of RM213–244 is **2.5–3x higher than Meta LG**. For 12% of budget it only delivers 5.2% of leads. The MBA creative shortage noted in the strategy doc is an additional blocker to scaling this channel. TikTok TOF spend should be justified by downstream conversion data, not assumed.

## 3. State-by-State Analysis: IPTA Competition vs Ad Performance

### The Terengganu–Kelantan Paradox (Confirmed by Data)

| Metric | Kelantan | Terengganu |
|---|---|---|
| IPTA students | 15,006 (UMK only) | 25,975 (UniSZA + UMT) |
| IPTS NE | 2,965 (2024) | 1,421 (flat YOY) |
| UNITAR NE | 613 (2025) | 144 (2024) |
| Ad CPL | RM80 | RM69 |
| Ad spend | RM22,403 | RM13,235 |

Terengganu has a **lower CPL** than Kelantan but a **much smaller addressable market** due to dual state university competition. The algorithm will always prefer Terengganu on efficiency metrics — but the actual enrolment ceiling is far lower. This is a strong argument for the separate campaign structure with volume floors by location.

### The Three Flagged Underperformers — Root Cause Confirmed

**Penang (RM240 CPL)**
- IPTA: USM (34,598 students, stable, -1.4% recent YOY)
- IPTS: 21,475 students, Management-dominated (4,685 enrolment)
- Market shrinking -16% YOY (PPTX slide 11)
- UNITAR Bayan Lepas is in an industrial zone — E&E and semiconductor employers, not student catchment
- TAR UMT in Tanjung Bungah dominates with 1,985 NE (31.8% share)
- UNITAR NE: 135 (2025) — small and predominantly diploma
- **Structural fix**: DHRM + Logistics/OSH for Bayan Lepas manufacturers; this is the right call but is a product/sales problem, not an ad problem

**Malacca (RM83 CPL — actually not a CPL problem)**
- IPTA: UTeM (15,593 students, bounced back +10.3% YOY)
- IPTS: 18,138 students — Law, Business Admin, IT dominate
- Market actually grew ~45% in 2024
- The strategy doc flags Malacca as underperforming, but the ad CPL (RM83) is the third-best in the portfolio
- Real issue: product positioning in a highly competitive multi-segment market; Jonker Street parking/accessibility is a conversion (not leads) problem
- Newer recommendation (slide 97): Close College, maintain RC (Cosmo)

**Kuching/Sarawak (RM155 CPL)**
- IPTA: UNIMAS (17,372 students, growing faster than Sabah)
- IPTS: 17,573 students, Management dominant (3,044)
- Market leaders are heavily subsidised (40% share)
- Key insight from PPTX slide 59/99: Need "University" branding, not "College" — RC is strategically more important than UC here
- Recommendation: Close College (Cosmo), retain RC, expand to Miri + Sibu via ODL

### High-Opportunity States (Underinvested)

**Kelantan (RM80 CPL, 613 NE in 2025)**
- IPTS market dominated by MARA (73% share) but UNITAR rank #3
- ECE is the massive segment: 3,119 IPTS enrolment, MARA alone does 938 NE in DECE
- UNITAR grew from 219 NE (2024) to 613 NE (2025) — a 180% jump
- UNITAR's issue: enrolled Bachelor-heavy when the market is Diploma-heavy — gap still exists
- Spend (RM22,403) is the second-lowest in the portfolio despite strong performance
- **Action**: Significant underinvestment — should be in the top-tier priority allocation

**Kedah / Sungai Petani (RM106 CPL)**
- UNITAR is the market leader in the Education vertical (per PPTX slide 9)
- UUM (state IPTA) declining at -5% CAGR and -6.2% recent YOY — demand shifting to IPTS
- B40 market — price sensitivity is real, but so is the opportunity vacuum
- Spend (RM40,445) is low for a campus where UNITAR holds market leadership

**Pahang/Kuantan (RM103 CPL)**
- IPTS: 18,332 students — Management dominant (5,115 enrolment in segment)
- UMPSA (IPTA) is contracting (-2.1% CAGR) — demand shifting
- Highly competitive (MARA, Yayasan Pahang, UniTEN) with similar programs/pricing
- Borderline case — Terengganu-Pahang border could share targeting per strategy doc

## 4. The "Cosmo" Restructure (PPTX Slides 88–99)

The updated recommendations (slides 88–99 vs 9–65) reveal a second, more radical strategy layer: a physical footprint restructure under a "Cosmo" model. This is distinct from and complementary to the media strategy changes.

| State | Current | Proposed (Cosmo) |
|---|---|---|
| Kedah | ANS RC + SC Sg Petani | Merge SC → College (Cosmo), maintain RC; add Kulim ODL |
| Penang | SC Butterworth + UC Bayan Lepas | Maintain SC as sales office; College (Cosmo) Bayan Lepas |
| Perak/Ipoh | UC + RC | College (Cosmo) + RC; add Kampar RC (new), Taiping/Manjung ODL |
| Kelantan | ANS RC + UC | Maintain RC for Bachelor; College (Cosmo) for Diploma |
| Terengganu | UC + RC | College (Cosmo) KT; new Kemaman ODL |
| Pahang | UC + SC | College (Cosmo); close SC (merge in) |
| Melaka | UC + RC | Close College; maintain RC (Cosmo) |
| Johor | UC + RC + SC | College (Cosmo); maintain RC; close SC; new Kluang/Kulai ODL |
| Sabah | RC + potential | College (Cosmo) KK; new Tawau/Sandakan ODL |

**Implication for media strategy**: If footprint is changing (closures, relocations, new ODL partners), the geographic targeting setup for C2 2026 needs to account for new catchment areas (Kemaman, Kluang, Kulai, Kulim, Tawau, Miri, Sibu) that don't currently exist in the media plan.

## 6. Integrated Strategic Risks

### Risk 1: Lead Volume Target vs Revenue Quality
The plan targets 41,503 leads at RM99 CPL. Moving to a UC-focus model will reduce lead volume significantly (removing or capping Selangor dominance) while raising blended CPL. The strategy doc acknowledges "moving away from 34,000 lead volume target" — but the media plan has not been updated to reflect the new UC-weighted structure. The numbers in the plan are based on the old model.

### Risk 2: Algorithm vs Geography Conflict
Meta and Google will continue to optimise toward Selangor/Kelana Jaya (RM36 CPL) unless forced. Separate campaign structures per location tier are necessary but will reduce platform learning efficiency and require ~2–4 weeks of re-learning per campaign. The March 20% spend ramp is tight for a restructure of this complexity.

### Risk 3: Cosmo Footprint Changes Mid-Cycle
If campuses close or relocate during C2 2026, geographic targeting, landing pages, and conversion attribution all need updating mid-flight. This should be sequenced before campaign launch, not during.

### Risk 4: Kelantan Underinvestment
Kelantan has the third-best CPL (RM80), is growing fast (180% NE YOY), and has an identified structural advantage over Terengganu (less IPTA competition). Yet it receives the second-lowest ad budget. Without guaranteed budget floors in the new campaign structure, the algorithm will continue to ignore it in favour of Selangor.

## 7. Recommended Prioritisation Framework

Based on combined ad performance, IPTA/IPTS competition, UNITAR NE trajectory, and product fit:

**Tier 1 — Over-index (UC Priority)**
- Kelantan (strong NE growth, market gap, low IPTA competition, ECE demand)
- Kedah/Sg Petani (market leader in Education, UUM declining, relatively low spend)
- Pahang/Kuantan (medium CPL, UMPSA contracting, ECE + Business opportunity)

**Tier 2 — Maintain with UC-specific campaigns**
- Sabah/KK (medium CPL, ECE strong, growing IPTS)
- Perak/Ipoh (Foundation opportunity, PTPTN advantage, no dominant non-science player)
- Johor/JB (highest CPL, but large addressable market; product fix needed alongside media)

**Tier 3 — Structural fix first, then media**
- Penang (product/location problem; wait for DHRM/Logistics launch)
- Sarawak/Kuching (branding problem; RC > UC; media is secondary to footprint decision)
- Malacca (closure decision pending; don't over-invest before Cosmo restructure)
- Terengganu (CPL looks good but enrolment ceiling is real; cap spend, don't scale)

**Remove from volume targeting**
- Selangor KL (let algorithm run for volume but exclude from UC-focus homegrown tracking)
- KL (RM179 CPL is expensive for a market crowded with alternatives)
