# UNITAR – Master of Education (1-Year Master's for Working Educators)

## Project Name
UNITAR – Master of Education (1-Year Postgrad, EPF-Eligible)

## Project Description
UNITAR International University is recruiting working educators into the Master of Education (by Research) at its UUCKL campus in Kuala Lumpur. Non-physical product (postgraduate education programme). Target audience: 25–40-year-old Malaysian teachers, kindergarten/college lecturers, and education-adjacent professionals who already have a bachelor's degree and want a Master's to move into senior teaching, lecturer, or education-policy roles — without quitting their job. Collaboration goal: drive enquiries to UNITAR via unitar.my/request-info.

## Product Description
A 1-year (3-semester) MQA-accredited Master of Education at UNITAR University College Kuala Lumpur (UUCKL), on Jalan Perak. It's a research-based Master's — instead of more lecture classes, students work on their own original research project on a real education topic with guidance from a supervisor. Designed to fit around a working educator's schedule. EPF (KWSP) withdrawal is approved, so students can fund it using their retirement savings. Fees from RM 16,320.

## Product Selling Points
- **Finish your Master's in just 1 year** (3 semesters) — built to fit around a full-time teaching or work schedule.
- **Pay with your EPF (KWSP)** — EPF withdrawal is approved for this programme, so you don't need to find the cash upfront.
- **Do real research on a topic you care about** — write your own thesis with a supervisor (good if you want to become a lecturer, move into policy, or eventually pursue a PhD).

## Preferred Creator Description
- **Identity:** Malaysian working educators aged 25–38 — school teachers (cikgu), tadika owners or teachers, college/university lecturers, training officers, education-NGO staff. Already hold a bachelor's degree.
- **Style:** "Day in my life as a cikgu", "What I'm doing after work to level up", honest talking-head storytime in BM or Manglish. Calm, sincere, not over-produced.
- **Audience:** Malaysian working educators (25–40), fresh education-degree grads wondering what's next, and people in HR / training / corporate-learning roles who want to move into formal education.
