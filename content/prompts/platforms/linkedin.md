# LinkedIn prompt scaffold

Platform-specific prompt patterns for LinkedIn organic + sponsored content. Reference `brain/voice/tone-by-channel.md` for voice.

---

## Tone

- Professional but not corporate. Conversational, opinion-led.
- First-person ("I", "we") outperforms third-person on the feed.
- Specific > generic. Numbers and named cases beat abstractions.

---

## Length norms

| Post type | Length | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Text post | 100–250 words | First 3 lines decide click-to-expand |
| Long-form (article) | 800–1,500 words | Use for thought-leadership |
| Document (carousel PDF) | 8–12 slides | High dwell time, algorithm rewards |
| Video post | 30–90 seconds | Captions burned-in |
| Poll | 4 options max | Drives comments + reach |

---

## Hook patterns

1. **Counter-intuitive claim** — "[Common wisdom] is wrong. Here's what works instead."
2. **Personal stake** — "I [outcome] in [time]. Here's exactly what I did."
3. **Mistake confession** — "I made a $X mistake last year. Here's what I learned."
4. **List promise** — "5 things I wish I knew about [topic] when I started."
5. **Question** — "What's the one [thing] that [outcome]? Mine: [answer]."

---

## Structure (text post)

1. Hook (line 1) — clickable, no jargon.
2. Whitespace break.
3. Context (2–3 lines) — why you're qualified to say this.
4. Body (the substance) — short paragraphs, line breaks.
5. Close (1 line) — takeaway or question.
6. Optional: hashtag block (3–5, niche over broad).

---

## What works

- Whitespace. Mobile-first formatting.
- Concrete numbers ("$240K", not "significant revenue").
- Comments-engagement loops (reply to every comment in the first hour).
- Posting Tue–Thu, 7–10am local time (sales/B2B audiences).

---

## What doesn't

- Buzzword salad ("synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem").
- External links in the post body (LinkedIn suppresses reach — put in first comment).
- "Agree?" sign-offs.
- Posting on weekends for B2B.
