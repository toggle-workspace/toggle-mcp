# YouTube Shorts prompt scaffold

Platform-specific prompt patterns for YouTube Shorts. Reference `brain/voice/tone-by-channel.md` for voice.

---

## Format rules

- Aspect: 9:16 vertical.
- Length: 15–60 seconds (Shorts cap).
- Captions: burned-in. YouTube auto-captions exist but are unreliable.
- Title: matters more than on TikTok — appears as part of the discovery surface.
- End screen: subscribe prompt + next-video link drives channel growth.

---

## Hook patterns

1. **Question hook** — "Why does [common thing] [unexpected outcome]?"
2. **Demo cold open** — show result in first second; explain after.
3. **List promise** — "Three things I learned about [topic] in 60 seconds."
4. **Pattern interrupt** — visual cut at 0.5s.
5. **Cliffhanger** — "Wait until you see what happened at the end."

---

## Structure

1. Hook (0–2s) — visual + verbal.
2. Payoff or premise (2–8s) — what the viewer will get.
3. Body (8–55s) — the substance.
4. Loop or CTA (55–60s) — either loop back to the hook (algorithm rewards completion + replay) or hard CTA.

---

## What works

- Loops. Designing the last frame to match the first drives replays.
- Personal channels > brand channels. Founders / creators outperform logos.
- Series formats ("Day X of [project]") build subscriber stickiness.
- Cross-posting from TikTok works but YouTube prefers native (no TikTok watermark).

---

## What doesn't

- TikTok watermark — algorithmically suppressed.
- Long intros. Subscribers come from completion, not greetings.
- Static talking-head with no B-roll for the full 60s.
- Auto-generated captions left unedited.
