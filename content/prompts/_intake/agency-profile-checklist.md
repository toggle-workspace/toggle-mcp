# Agency Inputs for Social Media Agent Training

The more specific the inputs, the better the outputs. Provide the following to train agents to write accurate, on-brand, compelling posts.

---

## 1. Agency Identity
- **Name, tagline, mission statement**
- **Niche** — what type of clients, what industry, what geography
- **What makes you different** — not generic ("results-driven") but specific ("we only take 3 clients at a time and guarantee response within 2 hours")
- **Origin story** — how and why you started, what problem you were solving
- **Founder background** — specific credentials, failures, wins, defining moments

---

## 2. Services & Offer
- Exact services with names you use internally
- Your core offer (how it's packaged, priced, what's included)
- What you don't do (positioning through exclusion is powerful)
- Process / methodology if you have a named one

---

## 3. Client Case Studies *(your best content fuel)*

For each case study, provide:
- Client type (industry, size) — no need for full name unless public
- The specific problem they had when they came to you (in their words if possible)
- The **before state** in numbers (spend, leads, revenue, CAC, ROAS, etc.)
- What you did (brief — 2-3 sentences)
- The **after state** in numbers
- Timeline
- Any direct client quote

---

## 4. Performance Data
- Best results you've ever produced for a client (specific metrics)
- Average results across your client base
- Platform breakdowns (Meta, Google, LinkedIn, TikTok — whatever you run)
- Benchmarks you regularly beat
- Any proprietary data or observations from managing multiple accounts

---

## 5. Talking Points / Content Pillars
- What opinions do you hold that most agencies won't say out loud?
- What mistakes do you see clients making most often?
- What advice does the industry give that you think is wrong?
- What do clients believe when they come to you that turns out to be false?
- What's the most counterintuitive thing you've learned?

---

## 6. Tone of Voice
- 3-5 adjectives that describe how you want to sound
- Brands or people whose tone you admire
- Things you never want to sound like (corporate? bro? overly academic?)
- Any words, phrases, or terms you always or never use

---

## 7. Audience
- Who are you talking to? (Founder? CMO? In-house marketer? Business owner?)
- What keeps them up at night?
- What do they google at 11pm?
- What's their biggest fear about hiring an agency?

---

## 8. Proof & Social Capital
- Awards, press mentions, podcast appearances
- Recognizable brand names you've worked with (even anonymized)
- Team size, years in business, total ad spend managed
- Email list size, social following, any community you run

---

## 9. Platform-Specific Context
- Which platforms are you currently on / want to grow
- Existing post examples you like from your own account
- Posts you've made that performed well or poorly and why you think
