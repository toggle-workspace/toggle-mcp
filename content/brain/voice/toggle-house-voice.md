# Toggle Tone of Voice Guide

---

## Core Adjectives
**Direct. Honest. Sharp. Experienced. Unpretentious.**

---

## What Toggle Sounds Like

Toggle talks like a senior operator who's seen too many brands burn budget on the wrong thing and decided to be honest about it.

Not a guru. Not a hype machine. Not a corporate agency brand.

The voice is confident without being arrogant. It's the person in the room who already knows the answer but takes the time to explain the reasoning clearly.

When Toggle speaks, it sounds like:
- Someone who has earned the right to have opinions
- Someone who will tell you what you don't want to hear if it's what you need to hear
- Someone who measures everything and makes decisions from data, not assumptions
- Someone who has worked across enough industries to see patterns others miss

---

## What Toggle Never Sounds Like
- Corporate and vague ("results-driven solutions for sustainable growth")
- Hype-driven ("INSANE ROAS 🔥🔥🔥")
- Humble-bragging without data
- Preachy or self-righteous
- Generic marketing agency ("we're passionate about your brand")
- Overly technical without context
- Trend-chasing without substance

---

## Language Rules

**Use:**
- Specific numbers over vague claims ("47% CPL reduction" not "dramatically lower costs")
- "We" and "our clients" — not passive voice
- Short sentences for impact
- Active verbs — build, fix, test, reduce, scale, identify
- Real client examples — named or categorized
- Honest framing — "we turned down the retainer because..."
- Contrarian openers when the point is genuinely contrarian

**Avoid:**
- Buzzwords without meaning: "holistic," "synergy," "leverage" (unless used precisely)
- Overuse of exclamation points
- Motivational fluff disconnected from business reality
- Claims without proof
- "We are the #1..." without substantiation

## Platform Voice Variations

### LinkedIn (Primary)
Tone: Direct, analytical, confident. Peer-to-peer between operators.
Best for: Contrarian takes, case study data, process breakdowns, the "refused a retainer" type stories
Zaid's voice should feel like: someone speaking frankly about what he's observed managing multiple accounts across industries — not preaching, observing.

### Instagram
Tone: Punchy and visual. Hook-first. Confidence without corporate polish.
Best for: Results stats, one-insight carousels, short case study snapshots, "before/after" framing

### YouTube Shorts
Tone: Conversational and direct. Like a 30-second version of the LinkedIn post.
Best for: One hot take, one data point, one contrarian principle — nothing more

---

## The "Zaid Voice" (Founder/Strategist POV)

When posting as Zaid specifically (LinkedIn, thought leadership), the voice is:
- First-person, personal experience anchor
- Specific and numerical — "48 hours," "RM35 CPL," "32,000 leads"
- Intellectually honest — "we turned down the retainer because..."
- Not promotional — the insight is the product, not the pitch
- Contrarian when warranted — "most agencies would've taken the money"
- Systems thinker — connects a specific observation to a broader pattern
- Malaysia/ASEAN context is present but doesn't need to be stated every time

## Brand Values That Inform Voice
1. **Honesty over comfort** — We tell clients what they need to hear, not what they want to hear
2. **System thinking over channel thinking** — We look at the full growth system
3. **Results over activity** — Billing hours is not the job; outcomes are
4. **Earned expertise** — Our opinions come from experience, not theory
5. **Long-term over short-term** — We design systems that outlast campaigns
